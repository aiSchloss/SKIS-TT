Student: Parsa AMIN
Work: "School Sufferings" by Daniel Pennac

Instructions: Please answer the following questions based on your reading of the text.

1. The author describes his experience from a "twofold kind." What are these two perspectives, and how do they shape the narrative of the book?

2. In the first section, the author uses the metaphor of the "Djibouti Dump." What does this metaphor represent in the context of his childhood and education?

3. Describe the author's mother's reaction to his academic struggles. How does her anxiety manifest throughout the story, even after he becomes successful?

4. Who was the first teacher to have a positive impact on the author, and what unconventional method did he use to engage the young Pennac in writing?

5. The author refers to himself as a "dunce" and a "cork." How did this self-perception affect his ability to learn and his interactions with teachers and family?

6. Besides his first "savior," the author mentions three other teachers who helped him. What were their respective subjects, and what common quality did they share in their teaching approach?

7. The author states that love was a major catalyst for his transformation. Describe the first significant romantic relationship he mentions and explain how it changed his perception of himself and his abilities.

8. What is the author's view on the role of reading? How did his personal, often secret, relationship with books contrast with his formal schooling?

9. In the anecdote about the trigonometry lesson with the dog, what does the author learn about intelligence and perception from the dog's actions?

10. Towards the end of the text, the author returns to the theme of the "dunce" by confronting his own inner critic. What does this internal dialogue reveal about the long-lasting impact of his school sufferings?
